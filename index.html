Understanding Food Insecurity

Food insecurity is the state of being without reliable access to a sufficient quantity of affordable, nutritious food. It affects millions of people around the world and can lead to serious health, social, and economic consequences.

  • Poverty and unemployment
  • Natural disasters and climate change
  • Conflict and displacement
  • Rising food prices
  • Inequitable food systems

Food insecurity can lead to malnutrition, poor academic performance in children, mental health issues, and chronic illnesses. It also deepens existing inequalities within communities.

  • Donate to or volunteer at local food banks
  • Support policies that reduce poverty and hunger
  • Reduce food waste
  • Educate others about the issue
  • Support local food banks
